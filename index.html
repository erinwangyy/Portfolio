Unconsciously or intentionally, I became an easily missing person, resulting from an unstoppable urge to go on journeys alone. Maybe any companionless traveler is originally a missing person that, for some reason, return. My traveling story is not an exception, which begins with getting lost, and starts from a departure.

On the Edge of Elapsing is a photo project exploring limbo time in life and the sticky transition between past, present, and future. From a long-term perspective, it rescues sentiments about growth as fragments of a life-assigned self-portrait.
